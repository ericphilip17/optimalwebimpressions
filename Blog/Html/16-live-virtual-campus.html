Live Virtual Campus feat Tech Adaptica


Since the strike of the deadly COVID-19 pandemic, governments from all over the globe have adopted stern measures to restrain human interaction and combat its spread. A common course of action has been to shut down all learning institutions.


As a result, many institutions have embarked on e-learning to keep their students studying. This is a great course. But wait, how about the loneliness, boredom, and isolation that students experience during these online classes?


In a bid to respond to these uncertainties, many technology experts have come up with worthwhile projects aimed at solving the challenges. One of the most transformative solutions has been the development of Live Virtual Campus platforms. A typical example of these platforms has been developed by Tech Adaptica, an Information Technology company based in Toronto, Canada.


At Yale University, for instance, the administration embraced online learning via Zoom and Canvas since the lockdown started. However, students still feel isolated in their homes because the meet-ups are just virtual. If we adopted Live Virtual Campus learning, the experience would be astounding.


So, what makes Live Virtual Campus unique?


Unlike in conventional online courses where learners access pre-recorded videos from their trainers, live virtual campus integrates both synchronous and asynchronous learning. Synchronous learning implies that students learn through real-time engagement with their trainers while in asynchronous learning, students access learning resources available on the platform for personal studies—without real-time coaching.


Why should schools switch to the Live Virtual Campus?


Essentially, most Live Campus platforms incorporate the following main features, which make learning more effective.


• A learning portal
This acts as a pool of resources where students can access all the relevant learning materials. Basically, this part is included to facilitate asynchronous learning.

• An assignments page
Once the professor is through with the lecture, they can issue an assignment immediately from this page. Also, students have a chance to discuss the assignment amongst themselves.

• Capacity to integrate third party learning portals
In many cases, a school administration will need to expand the available learning resources by adding third party learning portals such as Udemy and Coursera. Live Virtual Campus supports this feature.

• Enhanced social interaction
The greatest challenge in all other virtual arenas has always been the isolation of learners. A live virtual campus allows social interaction between learners and their professors by initiating a classroom setting. A live virtual campus can also include meeting rooms where students meet their professors or hold meetings amongst themselves.

• Avatars
The design of Live Virtual Campus resembles that of most video games, where students are replaced by avatars. Students will enjoy controlling avatars like they do when playing video games. In the end, they actually attend these lectures and engage in academic discussions, hence learning in the process.


In conclusion, learning institutions should endeavor to embrace live campus platforms for their students. This will go a long way in ensuring that their students are fully engaged in books and also interacting with their fellows.
