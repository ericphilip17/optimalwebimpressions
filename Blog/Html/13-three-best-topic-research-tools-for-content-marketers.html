3 Best Topic Research Tools for Content Marketers


Whether you are a "free bird" content writer like me or you have already narrowed down to the niche you love, there's always a reason to be picky with the content you publish on your website.


And like you would guess, it all comes down to your target audience. What exactly does your audience want to know? What are their pain points? Address that!


Sometimes using Google's "People also ask" results does the trick, but you need to be a bit more specific. And that's where dedicated topic research tools come in handy.


Haven't used one yet? Let me introduce you to a few.


#1: UBERSUGGEST

It deserves this first spot. As a content marketer, I'm pretty sure topic seasonality trend has a special place in your heart.


Whether you are writing short-lived content or building a long-term strategy for your brand, topic seasonality helps you figure out what topics are relevant so you can major on that.


Ubbersuggest shows you the trend as well as related keywords to your primary keyword


Even though the free version has a limited number of daily searches, it's still a great fit for a solo freelancewriter.


Fact: Ubbersuggest is 90–95% accurate.


#2: ANSWER THE PUBLIC

Answer The Public allows you to enter one or two primary keywords, and on searching, it throws back an extensive list of topics related to those keywords.


Your topics are grouped into four categories—as Questions, Prepositions, Comparisons, and Alphabetical. Additionally, you can download the list into a CSV file and share it with your team. You are at liberty to choose what works best for you.


And just like Ubbersuggest, the free version comes with limited daily searches.


#3: KEYWORDS EVERYWHERE

Google's Keywords Everywhere is a free browser extension that shows you related topics for everything you search—on the right-hand side of your SERP.


You only need to install the add-on on your browser, and you're good to go.

• Trend data for the topic/keyword you searched
• Related keywords
• People also search for
• Related long-tail keywords


#4: BONUS TOPIC RESEARCH TOOLS

• Questions.NINJA
• Topicseed
• Sense2vec


THE TAKE AWAY: EMBRACE TOPICAL SEO

For the most part, search engines today use topical SEO and long-tail keywords to know the relevance of a web page. So don't choose your topics blindly.


Consider adding these little topic research tools to your contentmarketing toolbox and map out your way to online success.
