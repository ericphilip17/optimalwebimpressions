Scary Times As 4 US Retail-Giants Report Bankruptcy


With consumers cutting off spending, government restricting movements, and physical stores closing down indefinitely, it almost feels like the end of business for some mega retail stores. A couple of months down the line, different enterprises have come out to file for bankruptcy so as to have an agreement with their creditors.


Here’s a list of four major brands to close down


J. Crew Group Inc.

The American retail company popular for preppy fashions such as khakis, jeans, and other high-end wearables reported this on March 4th, just after an employee succumbed to COVID-19. When filing their case at the Virginia federal court, the company requested to exchange ownership for its USD 1.65 billion debt to creditors.


Neiman Marcus

The century-old luxury products company couldn’t see this coming until May 7th. In a statement by CEO Neiman Marcus, the Dallas-based chain confirmed the company was indeed facing the unprecedented downsides of COVID-19, just like other brands. Fortunately, the company already accumulated about USD 675 million to aid in its process of restructuring.


JCPenney

CEO Jill Soltau reported that the hard reality hammering the American retail space compelled the company to switch their strategies to accommodate the changes. However, the CEO admitted that the enterprise had been experiencing financial downturns for a couple of years.


Hertz

The pandemic could even shake a 100+ years-in-business car rental company! According to Kathryn Marinello, the former CEO, no business can survive without revenue. And of course, with a lockdown and pressing debts from previous years, only a suspension of business could do.


The future is still green

Filing for bankruptcy does not necessarily mean the brand is out of business entirely—NO. It is an opportunity to put all debts pending, restructure its operations, and device new strategies better that it had, in a bid to emerge stronger after the period.


So, that is precisely what is happening to these brands. After all, who has an idea of what their future may bring?
