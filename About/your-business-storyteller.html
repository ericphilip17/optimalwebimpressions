Hello! And how are you?


My name is Eric, and I am a passionate freelance content creator, student of life, and a lone thinker.


Over the last three years, I have been helping both small and midsized brands get their feet off the ground in the online space.


Shortly before getting into this industry, I was running a clothing store in my city. And one thing really struck me, something I have always carried on up to date.


“People hate being sold to, but they love buying”.


Perhaps you’ve also noticed that, right? Being too salesy or ‘pushy’ only makes your prospects skeptical about your services. And of course, that’s true to the old golden saying--“When the deal is goo good, think twice.”


You might be offering exclusive services or top-tier products, but how you present them to your customer determines how you thrive or survive in the market.


Long story short.


After trying and testing the principle with multiple clients, I realized they actually loved it, and their sales were skyrocketing. A pretty good example is GradeWriters, where I wrote content for the entire website.


The next pillar to my write-ups is visibility on the internet, the SEO bit.


I love it when my great mentor, Meghan Grant, puts it as--“Writing for the Internet.” Because indeed it is. Apart from playing around with words and hooking readers with exquisite content, how much traffic does it bring?


That’s where keyword research comes in. Writing stellar content that is dry of keywords is great, but it can only get your business so far.


Although there are many On-Page SEO metrics that contribute to the overall performance of a post, using the most relevant keywords generously pushes you a milestone away.


That’s why I keep all my write-ups SEO friendly. Whether you have your target keywords ready or unaware of how to go about the research, here I bring my skills.


—Some of my posts are featured on several authority sites such as EnterpriseDB, BlueSky IT Partners, and Freelancemom.com.


What niches do I specialize in?


Well, I consider myself a free bird. Even though I am pretty much on digital marketing, technology, and health/fitness, I research and write about anything.


So, why not contact me and let us get your content marketing ball rolling?
